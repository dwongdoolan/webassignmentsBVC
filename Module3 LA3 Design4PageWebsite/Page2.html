Ideas for Healthy Living:

Eat Healthy Meals and Snacks

PRACTICAL HABITS

  1. Plan a weekly menu and grocery list

    It saves you time and money by knowing what you're going to have for dinner and ensuring you'll have the ingredients on hand. It reduces multiple trips to the grocery store, reduces unplanned purchases and reduces food waste by buying what you need.

  2. Cook meals at home

    You will know what goes into your meals and can control the fat and sodium.

  3. Prepare snacks in advance

    Cut up vegetables and have them available for adding to lunches or for snacks. Stock a snack section in your pantry.

  4. Eat breakfast

    Breakfast is the most important meal of the day. It boosts your metabolism, provides energy and nutrients, helps stabilize your blood sugars and improves concentration and mood.

  5. Avoid sugary drinks and fruit juice

    Sugary drinks including energy drinks, fruit drinks, 100% fruit juice, soft drinks and flavored coffees have lots of sugar and little to no nutritional value. Although fruit juice has some of the benefits of the fruit (vitamins, minerals), it has more sugar than the fruit and less fiber. Drink more water, it promotes hydration without calories.

  6. Include your kids in picking new vegetables and fruits to try each week

    Figure out how to cook it or prepare it together. It engages their curiosity and encourages their interest in healthy food choices, food preparation and in consuming it, if you have a selective eater.

  7. Try different cooking methods for vegetables

    Some kids and some adults respond differently to texture, for example, some kids may prefer roasted cauliflower to raw cauliflower.

  8. Try new recipes

    Variety is the spice of life and healthy doesn't have to be boring. You can learn about new ingredients or cooking techniques. Look in your existing cookbooks, try online sources or buy some cooking magazines for inspiration.

  9. Remake your favourite recipes

    One way to get kids engaged in meal planning is to encourage them to request their favourite recipes which often become "Family Classic Recipes" in our home.

  10. Plan for leftovers

    Try batch cooking or when making a casserole, double the recipe so you can eat one and freeze one for another meal.

  11. Eat the rainbow

    In addition to eating sufficient servings of fruits and vegetables, eating the rainbow is a great way to ensure you're eating a variety of fruits and vegetables throughout the day. You'll get a broader range of nutrients from natural sources and more health benefits.

What does eating healthy meals and snacks look like?


  • Eat more fruits and vegetables

    Aim for half your plate

    Packed with nutrients (antioxidants, vitamins, minerals and fibre) vegetables and fruits help you maintain a healthy weight by keeping you full longer.*


  • Choose whole grain options

    Aim for a quarter of your plate

    Sources include whole grain bread and crackers, brown or wild rice, quinoa, oatmeal and hulled barley, prepared using the entire grain. Whole grain foods have fibre, protein and B vitamins to help you stay healthy and full longer.*


  • Eat protein sources

    Aim for a quarter of your plate

    Sources include legumes, nuts, seeds, tofu, fortified soy beverage, fish, shellfish, eggs, poultry, lean red meats, lower fat milk, lower fat yogurts and cheeses lower in fat and sodium.**
*Heart and Stroke Foundation of Canada **Canada's Food Guide
