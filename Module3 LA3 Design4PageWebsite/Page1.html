Make Your Life Better:

Ideas for Healthy Living

Why have a healthy lifestyle?


Living a healthy lifestyle can contribute to an overall sense of happiness and well being and can help prevent chronic diseases and long-term illness. Making choices and developing habits can contribute to your mental and physical health.

11 THINGS YOU CAN DO TODAY

  1. Drink more water

    Water keeps every system in the body functioning properly. It promotes cardiovascular health, contributes to increased athletic performance, reduces joint pain, helps with weight loss, reduces headaches, balances your blood sugar and cleans toxins from your body and promotes healthy skin.

  2. Eat healthy meals and snacks

    Eating a healthy, balanced diet is one of the most important things you can do to protect your health. Healthy eating helps us to maintain a healthy weight and reduces our risk of type 2 diabetes, high blood pressure, high cholesterol and the risk of developing cardiovascular disease and some cancers.

  3. Be physically active

    Regular physical activity is one of the most important things you can do for your health. Being active improves your brain health, weight management, reduces disease, strengthens your bones and muscles and improves your ability to do everyday activities.

  4. Go to bed earlier tonight

    Good quality sleep reduces the risk of chronic life-threatening diseases. Sufficient sleep has a positive impact on learning and memory, provides more energy, strengthens the immune system, reduces anxiety and depression, sharpens thinking skills and increases productivity.

  5. Take control of your money

    Money gives you freedom and choices. Get informed, set financial goals, calculate your net worth, create a spending plan/budget, pay down debts, save and invest and understand your taxes.

  6. Declutter

    Decluttering and simplifying are about getting rid of the excess and the distractions so you have more time, space, energy and freedom for what matters most to you. Decluttering your work and living spaces helps your brain focus.

  7. Read more books

    Reading is like exercise for your brain. Reading books has many benefits, including, growing vocabulary, improving memory, developing stronger analytical thinking skills, improving focus and concentration, in addition to the knowledge gained.

  8. Be grateful

    When we acknowledge what is good in our lives it helps us experience more positive emotions. Gratitude can help you appreciate what you have rather than focusing on what you don't have. It helps you savour good experiences, deal with adversity and build strong relationships.

  9. Develop stress management techniques

    Positive stress can help us grow. Negative stress can hold us back, cause anxiety and be perceived as outside of our coping abilities. Effective stress management is important in a balanced life, a tool in developing resilience to thrive under pressure and address challenges. Think about practicing yoga, meditation or deep breathing exercises.

  10. Challenge your mind

    A healthy brain thrives on challenge, especially challenges that are personally relevant and involve many different parts of the brain at the same time. If you have a challenge it gives a sense of purpose and a sense of accomplishment when you master a task. Learn something new, volunteer your time and share your knowledge or rediscover a childhood hobby or interest.

  11. Build a strong social network

    Humans are social creatures, the number and quality of your relationships play a significant role in our mental and emotional health. Relationships with your friends, family and community can be a great source of support in adversity and foster a sense of connection. The people you spend the most time with affect your behaviours so choose friends with healthy habits!
